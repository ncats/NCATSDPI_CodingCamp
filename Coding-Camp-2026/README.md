# NCATS Spring Coding Camp 2026

## Course Goals
The goal of the camp is to support participants in learning core coding concepts through guided instruction. It is designed for both beginners and experienced programmers, and no prior experience is required. By the end of the program, participants will be introduced to Python, R, GitHub, and utilization of vibe coding (Codex) in programing. Based on their starting level, participants are expected to make meaningful progress in their skills and deepen their expertise in a programming language or specific application of their choice.

### **Why Join Coding Camp?**

- Gain **hands-on coding experience** while exploring key biomedical data platforms that support translational science and research  
- Build a **codebase or informatics pipeline** to address a real-world challenge, with guidance from an experienced TA  
- **New this year:** Learn how to incorporate **AI-assisted coding** into your workflow  
- Get exposure to **DPI-supported platforms and resources** (e.g., RDAS, Pharos, RaMP-DB, KNIME/Python workflows, APIs, etc.)  

---

### **Program Structure**

- **Weekly group sessions**  
  - Live demos and hands-on exposure to DPI-supported tools and resources  

- **Weekly TA meetings**  
  - Collaborate with your assigned TA to develop your project  

- **Final two weeks**  
  - Dedicated to **project presentations**  

---

### **Final Presentation**

- Each participant will deliver a **~15-minute presentation**  
- Share the results and impact of your project
